Content Development Strategy

A structured approach to content development ensures your chatbot provides consistent, effective support for Medicaid members.

Layered Knowledge Structure

Create training content in three distinct layers:

Layer 1: Fundamental Concepts

  • Basic definitions of employment terms
  • Simple explanations of the job search process
  • Clear descriptions of portal features

Example: "A resume is a document that shows your work history and skills. It helps employers learn about you."

Layer 2: Procedural Guidance

  • Step-by-step instructions for tasks
  • Visual references when possible
  • Frequent encouragement at each step

Example: "To start your resume: 1. Click the 'Resume' button at the top. 2. Choose 'Create New Resume.' 3. Fill in your name and contact info. Great job! Now let's add your work history."

Layer 3: Supportive Resources

  • Information about support services
  • Guidance for overcoming barriers
  • Connections to additional resources

Example: "If you need help getting to a job interview, you might qualify for transportation assistance. Would you like to learn more about transportation help?"

Implementation Tasks:

  1. Audit existing content and categorize into these three layers
  2. Identify gaps in each layer
  3. Prioritize content development to fill these gaps
  4. Create a review process to ensure all three layers are maintained

Content Format Templates

Create standardized templates for different types of training content:

Q&A Pairs Template:

User Question: [Simple version of question]
Alternative Phrasings: [3-5 different ways users might ask]
Response: 
- Opening: [Friendly acknowledgment]
- Answer: [Simple explanation at 5th-grade level]
- Action Step: [Clear next step]
- Encouragement: [Positive reinforcement]

Process Guide Template:

Task Name: [Feature or process name]
User Trigger Phrases: [Phrases that should activate this guide]
Introduction: [Simple explanation of the task's purpose]
Steps:
1. [First step with encouragement]
2. [Second step with encouragement]
3. [Third step with encouragement]
Completion: [Celebration and suggestion for next action]

Feature Introduction Template:

Feature Name: [Name of portal feature]
Value Statement: [How this helps the user]
Simple Description: [5th-grade level explanation]
Getting Started: [First step to try the feature]
Success Example: [Story of how someone benefited]

Content Topics Priority List

Focus your initial training content on these high-priority areas:

1. Profile Completion

  • Explaining why a complete profile helps find better jobs
  • Step-by-step guidance for each profile section
  • Encouragement for completing "difficult" fields

2. Resume Creation

  • Breaking down resume creation into micro-steps
  • Handling employment gaps positively
  • Explaining how to highlight transferable skills

3. Job Search Navigation

  • Simple search strategies (based on the AND logic keyword system)
  • Understanding job listings and requirements
  • Saving and tracking job applications

4. O*NET Interest Survey

  • Explaining the purpose and value of the survey
  • Guidance for understanding and using results
  • Connecting interests to potential careers

5. Support Services Access

  • Clear explanation of available services
  • Eligibility information in simple terms
  • Step-by-step guidance for requesting assistance

Implementation Approach

1. Content Development Workflow

Follow this process for creating effective training content:

  • Identify Knowledge Gaps: Review conversation logs to find common questions
  • Draft Content: Create initial content using the templates
  • Review and Refine: Test with sample users from target demographic
  • Implement in Stages: Add content to training system in small batches

2. Reading Level Optimization

Techniques to maintain 5th-grade reading level:

  • Keep sentences under 15 words
  • Use common, everyday words
  • Avoid jargon and technical terms
  • Use active voice instead of passive
  • Break complex concepts into simple steps
  • Use concrete examples rather than abstract explanations

Example Transformation:

Before: "Employment opportunities can be identified through utilization of our comprehensive search functionality, which allows for filtering based on various criteria including location, required qualifications, and compensation expectations."

After: "You can find jobs using our search tool. You can look for jobs near you, jobs that match your skills, and jobs that pay what you need."

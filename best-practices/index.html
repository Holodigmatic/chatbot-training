Best Practices for Medicaid Member Audience

These recommendations are tailored to address the unique characteristics and needs of Medicaid members.

Conversation Design

1. Simplify Every Interaction

  • One Task, One Conversation: Focus each interaction on completing a single task
  • Micro-Steps Approach: Break even simple processes into very small steps
  • Visual Cues: Describe visual elements ("the blue button") to aid navigation
  • Confirmation: Confirm understanding before moving to next steps

Example:

Instead of: "Complete your profile by filling out the personal information, work history, education, and skills sections."

Use: "Let's start with your profile. First, let's add your name and contact info. Look for the form at the top of your profile page. Do you see where to type your name?"

2. Build Confidence Through Design

  • Start with Success: Begin interactions with something easy to build confidence
  • Celebrate Small Actions: Acknowledge every completed step, no matter how small
  • Normalize Challenges: Use phrases like "many people find this part tricky"
  • Provide Reassurance: Remind users they can save progress and return later

Example:

"Great job clicking on the Resume Builder! That's the first step. Many people find creating a resume takes a few sessions to complete. You can save your work at any time and come back to it when you're ready."

3. Maintain Engagement

  • Progress Indicators: Regularly mention how far along they are in a process
  • Curiosity Hooks: Use questions to maintain interest
  • Value Statements: Clearly explain why each action benefits them
  • Immediate Rewards: Highlight immediate benefits of completing steps

Example:

"You've finished 2 out of 5 sections of your profile! When you complete the skills section next, you'll immediately see jobs that match your abilities. This helps you find better job matches faster."

4. Address Common Barriers Proactively

  • Anticipate Obstacles: Proactively mention solutions to common barriers
  • Resource Connections: Link to support services at relevant moments
  • Alternative Paths: Always offer simpler alternatives when available
  • Recovery Routes: Provide clear paths to recover from errors or confusion

Example:

"I notice you're looking at jobs that require transportation. Did you know that Medicaid might help with transportation to interviews and work? Would you like to learn about transportation assistance programs?"

5. Optimize for Cognitive Accessibility

  • Consistent Patterns: Use the same conversation patterns repeatedly
  • Limited Options: Present no more than 2-3 choices at once
  • Memory Aids: Repeat important information throughout the conversation
  • Distraction Mitigation: Keep conversations focused on the immediate task

Example:

"Let's focus on just one thing right now: adding your most recent job to your resume. Don't worry about anything else yet. What was your last job title?"

Technical Implementation

1. Response Timing and Length

  • Quick Initial Response: Acknowledge questions immediately
  • Chunked Information: Deliver information in small, digestible chunks
  • Progressive Disclosure: Reveal additional details only when needed
  • Optimal Length: Keep responses under 50 words when possible

2. Conversation Recovery Mechanisms

  • Confusion Detection: Implement patterns to detect when users are confused
  • Graceful Fallbacks: Create friendly responses for when the chatbot doesn't understand
  • Conversation Resets: Provide clear options to start over when conversations go off track
  • Human Escalation: Offer clear path to human support for complex situations

3. Personalization Strategy

  • Remember Basics: Store and reference key user information (name, job interests)
  • Preference Adaptation: Adjust conversation style based on user engagement
  • Progress Tracking: Reference previous accomplishments to build confidence
  • Contextual Awareness: Acknowledge return visits and continued progress

4. Testing with Representative Users

  • Diverse Test Group: Include users with varying literacy levels and disabilities
  • Observation Sessions: Watch real users interact with the chatbot
  • Simplified Feedback: Create very simple feedback mechanisms
  • Iterative Refinement: Make small, frequent improvements based on observations

Content-Specific Best Practices

1. Job Search Guidance

  • Keyword Simplification: Provide explicit guidance on the AND logic search system
  • Expectations Setting: Help users understand typical application timelines
  • Application Tracking: Encourage saving and tracking applications
  • Rejection Resilience: Offer supportive responses for job search challenges

Example:

"When searching for jobs, try using just one word at a time, like 'warehouse' instead of 'warehouse jobs.' Our search looks for all the words you type, which sometimes means fewer results when you use multiple words."

2. Resume Building Support

  • Strength Identification: Help users identify transferable skills from life experience
  • Gap Explanation: Provide positive framing for employment gaps
  • Achievement Extraction: Guide users to identify achievements in previous roles
  • Simplification Techniques: Offer templates and examples at appropriate reading levels

Example:

"Even if you haven't had many jobs, you have valuable skills! Did you care for family members, volunteer, or manage your household? These all use important skills employers want, like responsibility and problem-solving."

3. Interview Preparation

  • Basic Concepts: Explain fundamental interview expectations
  • Simple Preparation: Break preparation into very small, manageable tasks
  • Confidence Building: Provide reassurance and positive framing
  • Practical Logistics: Address transportation, clothing, and other practical concerns

Example:

"Before your interview, try practicing just one question: 'Tell me about yourself.' You only need a few sentences about your background and why you want the job. Would you like some simple examples to help you practice?"

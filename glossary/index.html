Glossary of AI Chatbot Terms

This glossary provides definitions for key terms related to AI chatbots, ensuring everyone on your team has a common understanding of the technology.

API (Application Programming Interface)

A set of rules that allows different software applications to communicate with each other. In your case, your chatbot uses OpenAI's API to access the GPT-4 model.

Base Prompt

The foundational instructions given to an AI model that guide how it should respond. This includes information about its identity, purpose, and communication style.

Chatbot

A computer program designed to simulate conversation with human users, especially over the internet.

GPT-4

A large language model developed by OpenAI that powers your chatbot. It's trained on vast amounts of text data and can generate human-like responses to questions and prompts.

MCO (Managed Care Organization)

In your context, this refers to your largest client, a healthcare organization that manages Medicaid benefits for its members.

Prompt Engineering

The practice of designing and refining the instructions (prompts) given to AI models to optimize their responses for specific purposes.

RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)

A technique that enhances AI responses by first retrieving relevant information from a knowledge base and then using that information to generate more accurate, specific responses. Think of it as giving the AI access to a specialized reference library.

Reading Level

A measure of the complexity of text. A 5th-grade reading level means the text should be understandable by an average 5th-grade student (typically 10-11 years old).

Training Data

The information provided to a chatbot to help it learn about specific topics or how to respond in certain situations. This can include files, website content, or directly input text.

User Journey

The path a user takes when interacting with your platform, from initial engagement through various features to achieving their goals (like finding employment).

Natural Language Processing (NLP)

The branch of artificial intelligence that deals with the interaction between computers and humans using natural language. NLP enables chatbots to understand, interpret, and respond to human language.

Intent Recognition

The ability of a chatbot to understand what a user is trying to accomplish with their query, even when phrased in different ways.

Entity Extraction

The process of identifying and extracting specific pieces of information (entities) from user input, such as dates, locations, or job titles.

Conversation Flow

The structured path of a conversation between a user and a chatbot, including decision points, responses, and potential branches based on user input.

Fallback Response

A default response provided by a chatbot when it doesn't understand a user's query or can't provide a specific answer.
